I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Stanford University, advised by Kunle Olukotun. Before Stanford, I earned my bachelor's degree from the University of Chicago, where I worked with Junchen Jiang and Ravi Netravali on video analytics systems.

My research focuses on systems and algorithms for post-training and inference-time adaptation of large language models (LLMs) and agents. I build self-improving AI systems that close the loop between model inference, reflection, and systems optimization. My recent work includes ACE, FlowRL, APC (NeurIPS 2025), and Caravan (OSDI 2024)—frameworks that combine scalable systems design with learning-based adaptation, enabling large models to reason efficiently and evolve autonomously.

My first name, Qizheng, is pronounced like "keygen" in public key encryption. I also go by Alex.

I am a fourth-year CS Ph.D. student at Stanford University. I am a member of [Stanford Pervasive Parallelism Lab](https://ppl.stanford.edu/) and [LMCache](https://github.com/LMCache/LMCache/).
